Tamilnadu - state of temple

Tamil Nadu is renowned for its temple towns and heritage sites and has some of the most remarkable temple architecture in the country. Tamil Nadu is the most popular state for tourism in the country and a living tradition of music, dance and folk arts. Famous temples of Tamil Nadu includes Meenakshi Amman Temple,Great Living Chola Temples,Group of Monuments in Mahabalipuram and famous Lord Shiva Temples

Maharastra -monsoon destination

Maharashtra state is the most visited state by domestic tourists in country and attracts many tourists from different states and foreigners too. Aurangabad is the tourism capital of Maharashtra and other major urban centers include Mumbai, Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Nanded and Nagpur. The geography of Maharashtra shows its divine nature in full glory, especially during the monsoon season and when every destinations offer natural landscape and better holiday experience.

Uttarpradesh -popular state for tourist

Uttar Pradesh has a large number of historical monuments and places of religious significance. The state is one of the most popular and an established tourist destination for both Indians and international tourists and also home of India’s most visited sites, the Taj Mahal, and holiest city of Varanasi

Karnataka – Historical Monuments State

Karnataka ranked as the fifth most popular state in India for tourism and home to 507 of the 3600 centrally protected monuments in India. Tourism in Karnataka can be divided as Hill Stations,Coastal town,ancient temples and protected monuments, The most popular protected monuments of Karnataka includes Virupaksha Temple Hampi,Group of Monuments at Pattadakal, Chennakesava Temple,Mysore Palace,Badami cave temples and temple groups at Aihole.

Rajasthan – State of Breathtaking Views

Rajasthan is the most popular tourist destination in India for both domestic and international tourists and part of the Golden Triangle for tourists visiting India. The Royal State attracts tourists for historical forts and palaces, that includes lakes palaces of Udaipur,palaces of Jaipur and desert forts of Chittorgarh,Jodhpur, Bikaner and Jaisalmer.

Kerala – Best Family Holidays State

Kerala is one of the ten paradises in the world and one of the niche holiday destinations in India. The state is famous for for its ecotourism initiatives,hill stations,wildlife sanctuaries and beautiful backwaters. Kerala -God’s Own Country is one of the most visited tourism destinations in India.

Goa – Most Famous State in India

Goa is famous for its beaches,foreign tourist,places of worship,culture and a heritage museum. The smalles state of India host large number of both domestic and international tourists every year and major tourist attractions of Goa includes Bom Jesus Basilica, Fort Aguada, wax museum and the Churches and Convents of Goa heritage site.

Madhya Pradesh – State for Wildlife Tour

Madhya Pradesh is called as the Heart of India and was awarded as the best tourism state award in 2012. Tourism in Madhya Pradesh includes cultural heritage,carved temples, stupas and world heritage sites. Several cities in Madhya Pradesh are extraordinary for wildlife and scenic beauty such as Bandhavgarh National Park,Kanha National Park,Satpura National Park,Pench National Park and National Chambal Sanctuary..

Orissa – Emerging State for Tourism

The long coastline, temples, monuments,lakes and frolicking rivers of Odisha are the major tourist attractions in the country. Odisha is one of the major tourism sectors of India with various attractions and places such as temples of Bhubaneswar,beautiful beaches,Buddhist monuments,wildlife reserves, the arts and festivals.

Jammu and Kashmir – Best Place For Summer Snow

Jammu and Kashmir state of India is the most important tourist places of the country with number of natural paradise that includes the Mughal Gardens,houseboat on Dal Lake, snow capped mountains and valley of Kashmir. Tourist places of Kashmir also incudes Gulmarg,Sonmarg,Pahalgam and Srinagar.

Andhra Pradesh – Most Preferred Tourist State

The state of Andhra Pradesh is known as the Koh-i-Noor of India and has a variety of tourist attractions including sea beaches, rolling hills, natural caves, amazing wildlife and temples. Some of the tourist spots in Andhra Pradesh are beautiful beaches,Borra Caves,Araku Valley,Buddhist centres and five ancient Hindu temples of Lord Shiva..

Gujarat – Lion State of Western India

Gujarat is one of the most popular tourist regions in the country and visited by large number of domestic and international tourists. Tourism in Gujarat includes the hills of Saputara, Great Rann of Kutch,birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi and the only place in the world to see Indian Lions. The Khushboo Gujarat Ki campaign has increased the tourism in Gujarat and that covers Wildlife tourism,Religious tourism,Cultural tourism,heritage tourism and Medical tourism.

West Bengal – The State of Kingdoms

West Bengal has some of the marvelous landscape features,natural scenic beauty and India’s most preferred travel destinations like Darjeeling, Sundarbans,Sandakphu,Howrah Bridge,Great Banyan Tree and Bishnupur Temples. The state of West Bengal has significant architectural and natural heritage, that attracts tourists from around the world

Uttarakhand – Devbhumi of Indian States

Uttarakhand popularly known as land of Gods due to the presence of pilgrimage sites that includes Char Dham,Panch Badri,Panch Kedar,Panch Prayag and Haridwar. The state is also home to the first national park in India,Valley of Flowers,Kumbh Mela festival and hill stations. Most famous Hindu pilgrimage sites in Uttarakhand includes Kedarnath,Badrinath,Gangotri,Tungnath,Karnaprayag and Jageshwar.

Telangana – India’s 29th State

Telangana is one of the 29 states in India and ranked one of the best place in the world for tourist places that includes historical places, monuments, forts, water falls, forests and temples. Tourism in Telangana includes Charminar,Kakatiya Kala Thoranam,Golconda Fort,Yadagirigutta,Thousand Pillar Temple and Bhadrachalam Temple.

Bihar – Oldest Inhabited State

Bihar has a rich culture and heritage, known as one of the oldest inhabited places in the world. The state is dotted with innumerable ancient monuments and these tourist attractions visited by large numbers of tourists from all over the world, specially Mahabodhi temple,Asokan pillar,peace pagoda and Buddhist Stupa.

Punjab – Land Of Martyrs

Punjab is a well-known state in India that is located at the northwest end of the country. This state has gained recognition as the 'land of five rivers'. The five rivers are Sutlej, Ravi, Beas, Chenab and Jhelum. The state of Punjab acts as a clear demarcation of India from the neighbouring country Pakistan.

Delhi – Capital Of India

Delhi is of great historical significance as an important commercial, transport, and cultural hub, as well as the political centre of India. According to legend, the city was named for Raja Dhilu, a king who reigned in the region in the 1st century BCE. The names by which the city has been known—including Delhi, Dehli, Dilli, and Dhilli, among others—likely are corruptions of his name. .

Arunachal pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh, state of India. It constitutes a mountainous area in the extreme northeastern part of the country and is bordered by the kingdom of Bhutan to the west, the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, Myanmar (Burma) and the Indian state of Nagaland to the south and southeast, and the Indian state of Assam to the south and southwest. The capital is Itanagar..

Assams - State Of Tea garden

Assam, state of India. It is located in the northeastern part of the country and is bounded to the north by the kingdom of Bhutan and the state of Arunachal Pradesh, to the east by the states of Nagaland and Manipur, to the south by the states of Mizoram and Tripura, and to the west by Bangladesh and the states of Meghalaya and West Bengal. The name Assam is derived from the word asama, meaning “peerless” in the now extinct Ahom language..

Manipur

Manipur, state of India, located in the northeastern part of the country. It is bordered by the Indian states of Nagaland to the north, Assam to the west, and Mizoram to the southwest and by Myanmar (Burma) to the south and east. Like other northeastern states, it is largely isolated from the rest of India. The name Manipur means “land of gems.” Its economy centres on agriculture and forestry, and trade and cottage industries also are important..

Meghalaya - State Of Heavy Rain

Meghalaya, state of India, located in the northeastern part of the country. It is bounded by the Indian state of Assam to the north and northeast and by Bangladesh to the south and southwest. The state capital is the hill town of Shillong, located in east-central Meghalaya.One of the world’s wettest regions is found in Meghalaya—Cherrapunji, which has an average annual precipitation of about 450 inches (11,430 mm) during monsoon season (from May to September).

Mizoram

Mizoram, state of India. It is located in the northeastern part of the country and is bounded by Myanmar (Burma) to the east and south and Bangladesh to the west and by the states of Tripura to the northwest, Assam to the north, and Manipur to the northeast. The capital is Aizawl, in the north-central part of the state..

Nagaland

Nagaland, state of India, lying in the hills and mountains of the northeastern part of the country. It is one of the smaller states of India. Nagaland is bounded by the Indian states of Arunachal Pradesh to the northeast, Manipur to the south, and Assam to the west and northwest and the country of Myanmar (Burma) to the east. The state capital is Kohima, located in the southern part of Nagaland..

Tripura

Tripura, state of India. It is located in the northeastern part of the subcontinent. It is bordered to the north, west, and south by Bangladesh, to the east by the state of Mizoram, and to the northeast by the state of Assam. It is among the smallest of India’s states and is located in an isolated hilly region of the country, with various indigenous peoples—or tribes—accounting for a significant portion of the population. The capital is Agartala, near the Bangladesh border in the northwestern part of the state..
